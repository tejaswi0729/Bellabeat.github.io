Introduction

Welcome to the Bellabeat data analysis case study!The Bellabeat app provides users with health data related to their activity, sleep, stress,menstrual cycle, and mindfulness habits.The Bellabeat app connects to their line of smart wellness Products.

Scenario

I am a junior data analyst working on the marketing analyst team at Bellabeat, a high-tech manufacturer of health-focused products for women. Bellabeat is a successful small company, but they have the potential to become a larger player in the global smart device market. we have been asked to focus on one of Bellabeat’s products and analyze smart device data to gain insight into how consumers are using their smart devices. The insights we discover will then help guide marketing strategy for the company. we will present our analysis to the Bellabeat executive team along with our high-level recommendations for Bellabeat’s marketing strategy.

key stakeholders

  • Urška Sršen: Bellabeat’s cofounder and Chief Creative Officer
  • Sando Mur: Mathematician and Bellabeat’s cofounder; key member of the Bellabeat executive team
  • Bellabeat marketing analytics team: A team of data analysts responsible for collecting, analyzing, and reporting data that helps guide Bellabeat’s marketing strategy. I joined this team six months ago and have been busy learning about Bellabeat’’s mission and business goals — as well as how you, as a junior data analyst, can help Bellabeat achieve them.

Business Task

To focus on one of Bellabeat’s products and analyze smart device data to gain insight into how consumers are using their smart devices. The insights you discover will then help guide marketing strategy for the company.

preparing Data

  • The data has been made available by Mobius.
  • This Kaggle data set contains personal fitness tracker from thirty fitbit users. Thirty eligible Fitbit users consented to the submission of personal tracker data, including minute-level output for physical activity, heart rate, and sleep monitoring. It includes information about daily activity, steps, and heart rate that can be used to explore users’ habits.
  • Citation - The data is available on https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/arashnic/fitbit

Categorizing users based on their daily steps

from this article I have got some measures to categorize user’s based on their daily steps

  • Sedentary is less than 5,000 steps per day(<5000)
  • Low active is 5,000 to 7,499 steps per day(>5000 and <7500)
  • Fairly active is 7,500 to 9,999 steps per day(>7500 and <10000)
  • Active is more than 10,000 steps per day
type_of_user_by_steps <- daily_activity %>% 
  select(id, total_steps, calories) %>% 
  group_by(id) %>% 
  summarise(daily_average_steps = mean(total_steps), daily_average_calories = mean(calories)) %>% 
  mutate(user_type = case_when(
    daily_average_steps < 5000 ~ "Sedentary",
    daily_average_steps >= 5000 & daily_average_steps < 7500 ~ "Lightly Active",
    daily_average_steps >= 7500 & daily_average_steps < 10000 ~ "Fairly Active",
    daily_average_steps >=10000 ~ "Very Active"
  ))
